# 🔍 UX Reviewer — Starter Task

## What to send them

> Hey! Thanks for picking UX Reviewer. Here's your first 15-minute task.
>
> **Goal:** Use Astrion OS for 10 minutes and tell me the 3 most confusing things about it. Your job is to notice every tiny moment of "wait, what?" or "this is weird."
>
> **You don't need to install anything.** Just open this link in your browser:
> → https://viraajbindra-a11y.github.io/Astrion-OS/
>
> It's the web version — everything runs in your browser, no download needed.
>
> **What to do for 10 minutes:**
> 1. **Try to open Notes** — how long does it take you to find it?
> 2. **Try to change the wallpaper** — click around, see if you can figure it out
> 3. **Open Search** (press Ctrl+Space or Cmd+Space) and type "calculator"
> 4. **Try to close an app** — is the close button obvious?
> 5. **Just explore** — click everything that looks clickable
>
> **Then send me 3 moments that felt weird.** Examples:
> - "I couldn't find the volume control"
> - "The animation when I opened Notes was way too slow"
> - "I didn't know what the S1 thing in the corner meant"
> - "The settings button is hidden in a weird place"
> - "I clicked the brain thing and nothing happened"
>
> The weirder, the better. I want the stuff I'm BLIND to because I made it.
>
> No screenshots needed — just the words.

## What Viraaj does with the result

- For each piece of feedback, decide: is this a bug, a UX bug (it works but isn't obvious), or a matter of taste?
- UX bugs go in `tasks/ux-backlog.md` (create if it doesn't exist)
- Real bugs go in `tasks/lessons.md`
- **Reply thanking them specifically** — "you're right that the brain icon isn't obvious, I'm going to add a tooltip"
- Give them credit in the release notes for UX feedback

## Follow-up task

Ask them to do the same test in a month, after you've fixed the things they flagged. See if they feel the improvements. That closes the loop and gives them agency.
