# 💡 Ideas Person — Starter Task

## What to send them

> Hey! Thanks for picking Ideas Person. Here's your first 15-minute task.
>
> **Goal:** Send me **5 apps or features you wish existed** on a computer.
>
> That's the whole task. Seriously.
>
> **The rules:**
> 1. Don't censor yourself. Weird ideas are the best ideas.
> 2. Don't worry about whether it's "possible." That's my problem.
> 3. Don't just list apps that already exist (like "Spotify" or "Discord"). I want ideas for things that DON'T exist yet — or that exist but suck.
> 4. At least ONE of your ideas should be something kind of impossible or ambitious.
>
> **Examples to get you unstuck:**
> - "An app that tells me what to wear based on the weather AND my mood"
> - "A game where I fight bugs in my own code"
> - "A writing app that yells at me when I use the same word twice in a paragraph"
> - "A widget that shows how tired I look based on my webcam"
> - "A music player that auto-makes a playlist based on what I'm doing right now"
> - "An app that literally argues with me when I want to buy something dumb"
>
> **Format:** Just a list. One line per idea. Send them to me however.
>
> The good ideas will actually get built. I'm not joking.

## What Viraaj does with the result

1. Read the 5 ideas
2. For each, answer: **Can I build this? Is it a real app or a template? Does it need AI? What does the MVP look like?**
3. Pick the 1-2 best and add them to `tasks/app-ideas.md` (create if doesn't exist)
4. **If you build one of their ideas, CREDIT THEM in the release notes and the app's About dialog.** That's huge social reward.
5. Tell them which ideas you picked and why — they need to hear "your idea was good and is now happening"

## Follow-up task

Once they've seen one of their ideas turn into a real app in Astrion, their confidence goes up and they'll send better ideas next time. Ask them monthly. Build a culture where ideas are the most valuable thing.
